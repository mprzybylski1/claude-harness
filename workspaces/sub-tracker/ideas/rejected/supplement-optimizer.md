# Supplement Timing & Interaction Optimizer — REJECTED

> Rejected 2026-05-31. Fails stress test on data moat, retention, and AI substitution.

---

## The Idea

Native iOS app that tells you WHEN to take supplements relative to each other and food, warns about interactions (calcium blocks iron, D3 needs fat), and generates an optimized daily schedule. Differentiator over pill reminders: timing intelligence, not just adherence.

---

## Why It Was Rejected

### 1. The product already exists — multiple times — and none has broken out

| App | Launched | Reviews | Status |
|-----|----------|---------|--------|
| Supplements AI – Stack Tracker | Apr 2025 | 105 | Stalled |
| Staqc | 2024-2025 | New | Unproven |
| SuppCo | 2024 | 6000+ (Google) | Moderate traction |
| Supplemate | 2025 | Minimal | Brand new |

This is not a gap — it's a category with multiple recent entrants that all stalled. Suggests a market problem, not an execution gap.

### 2. The data moat is paper-thin

The entire "intelligence" layer is ~18 timing rules and ~12 interaction pairs. This is:
- Freely available from NIH Office of Dietary Supplements
- Fits on a single infographic
- Trivially replicable by any competitor in a weekend
- Not licensable/proprietary

### 3. AI directly substitutes the product

ChatGPT answers "when should I take my supplements?" with full personalization, citations, and schedule — for free, in one conversation. The static-rules nature means AI fully replicates the value prop.

### 4. "Look up once and you're done"

Once a user gets their optimized schedule, the core value is delivered. The schedule doesn't change. After setup, it's just a reminder app — competing against free iOS Reminders and Medisafe (5M users).

### 5. Care/of ($225M acquisition) shut down in 2024

Bayer acquired Care/of for $225M (2020), closed it June 2024 with 143 layoffs. The personalized-supplement-tech space is a graveyard.

### 6. Regulatory constraints weaken the differentiator

Strong warnings ("DO NOT take X with Y") push toward FDA medical device classification. Safe framing ("consider separating") is educational and weak. Stuck in positioning no-man's-land.

---

## Key Data Points

- US supplement users: 188M (75% of adults)
- Taking 3+ daily (actual target): 28-47M
- Care about timing AND will pay for app: 300K-1.4M (optimistic)
- Health app Day 30 retention: 5.7% average
- Core interaction database: ~30 rules total (not defensible)

---

## What Would Change This Assessment

- Integration with biomarker/blood work data (personalized beyond static rules) — but pushes deeper into medical device territory
- Practitioner channel (B2B via naturopaths) rather than direct consumer — different product entirely
- Supplement dispensary affiliate model (Fullscript earns 35% margins) — requires practitioner network
- Wearable integration showing measurable absorption outcomes — tech doesn't exist yet
